Why Solo Founders Burn Out

TL;DR: Burnout does not just come from working too much. It often comes from not knowing if what you are doing matters.

Some days you work for hours and feel fine. Other days you work just as hard and feel crushed. The difference is usually not workload. It is decision fatigue.

As a solo founder, you play two roles at once:

  • The Boss: decides the strategy.
  • The Employee: does the work.

Without structure, those roles interrupt each other constantly. You start a task, question whether it matters, get distracted, then return to it exhausted.

The burnout loop:
Task → Work → Doubt → Distraction → Exhaustion → "I did nothing today"

The solution is not more willpower. It is a system that separates strategy from execution.
