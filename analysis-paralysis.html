The "Analysis Paralysis" Tax

TL;DR: Thinking about your startup isn't work. If you can't map your next three steps in 90 minutes, you're paying a "tax" in lost time and mental energy.

Most founders think they are "refining" their idea. In reality, they are just paying the Analysis Paralysis Tax.

This tax is expensive. You pay for it with:

  • Market Relevance: While you're perfecting your logo, a competitor is talking to your future customers.
  • Mental Runway: Every day you spend "circling" an idea without acting, you lose a bit of the original excitement.
  • Actual Cash: Spending money on "nice-to-have" tools before you've validated the problem.
The 90‑Minute Rule Flowchart

┌─────────────────────────┐
│   Set a 90‑min timer    │
└───────────┬─────────────┘
            ▼
┌─────────────────────────┐

│    action tomorrow?     │
└───────────┬─────────────┘
            ▼
      ╔═══════════╗
      ║    YES    ║ ──► Start executing tomorrow
      ╚═══════════╝
            │
            ▼
      ╔═══════════╗
      ║    NO     ║ ──► Stop thinking. Get a structure.
      ╚═══════════╝

The Fix: Use the 90‑Minute Rule.

Set a timer. If you cannot clearly state who your customer is, what their pain is, and exactly what you are doing tomorrow morning to help them—stop "thinking." You don't need more thoughts; you need a structure.
