The "Analysis Paralysis" Tax

TL;DR: Thinking about your startup is not work. If you cannot map your next three steps, you are paying a tax in lost time and energy.

Most founders think they are refining their idea. In reality, they are often just paying the Analysis Paralysis Tax.

  • Market relevance: while you are polishing details, someone else is talking to real customers.
  • Mental runway: every day you circle an idea without acting, you lose some of the original energy.
  • Actual cash: money disappears into nice-to-have tools before the core problem is validated.
Set a clear limit:
- Who is the customer?
- What is the pain?
- What is the next action tomorrow?

If you cannot answer those clearly,
you do not need more thinking.
You need a structure.

The fix is simple: stop asking the same question in circles and create a decision structure that forces movement.
