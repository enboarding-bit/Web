Entrepreneurial Archetypes

Understand your natural tendencies and how to work with them.

Clarity

Your pain point: struggling to define or validate the right direction

Overthinker

Pattern: Thinking replaces doing.
Failure: Waiting for certainty that never arrives.
Key Tool: Decision constraint (limit options to force action).

Lost Executor

Pattern: Waiting for the right path.
Failure: Unused capacity because direction is unclear.
Key Tool: Clear‑path project charter (one‑page directive).

Starter

Pattern: Starting without finishing.
Failure: Loss of interest after novelty fades.
Key Tool: Idea‑to‑launch sprint (time‑boxed finish line).

Direction Stability

Your pain point: struggling to stay on one direction long enough for results to compound

Hesitator

Pattern: Protecting safety over growth.
Failure: Fear of loss prevents commitment.
Key Tool: Controlled experiment framework (small reversible bets).

Switcher

Pattern: Constant switching.
Failure: Never staying long enough for results to compound.
Key Tool: Compound experiment tracker.

Wanderer

Pattern: Avoiding commitment to keep options open.
Failure: Flexibility without direction.
Key Tool: Temporary constraint canvas (30‑day focus container).

Leverage

Your pain point: struggling to turn effort into meaningful results

Idealist

Pattern: Meaning without execution.
Failure: Hesitation when things feel commercial.
Key Tool: Value‑aligned business model canvas.

Busy Worker

Pattern: Activity without progress.
Failure: Effort misaligned with outcomes.
Key Tool: Strategic effort matrix.

Balanced Founder

Pattern: Steady but incremental progress.
Failure: Plateaus from lack of extreme leverage.
Key Tool: Scaling leverage audit.
